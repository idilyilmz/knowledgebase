## 1. Welcome to AI Ethics course!
- AI systems increasingly affect important areas such as social media, healthcare, finance, surveillance, and military operations
- while these technologies can provide benefits, they also pose risks and potential harms
- applying an ethical lens helps identify these harms and guides decisions about how to design, deploy, or even avoid building certain AI systems
- this course is **beginner-friendly**, with no prerequisites or programming experience required
- it is taught by Var Shankar and Alexis Cook and focuses on **practical, applied AI ethics**, including hands-on exercises with AI models and actionable guidance for teams building AI products

### a. Topics Covered
- **human-centered design**: Designing AI systems to meet real human needs
- **bias**: Understanding how AI systems can learn and reinforce discrimination
- **fairness**: Measuring and quantifying bias in AI models
- **model cards**: Using documentation to improve transparency and accountability

### b. Scope and Limitations
- AI ethics is an evolving field and an ongoing conversation
- while the course introduces core concepts, it does not cover all topics (e.g., economic inequality, job displacement, or authoritarian uses of AI)
- learners are encouraged to continue exploring these issues beyond the course


## 2. Key takeaway
ethical thinking is essential for responsible AI development, and this course provides a strong foundation for starting that conversation.
